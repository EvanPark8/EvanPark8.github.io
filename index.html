Who Powers the Energy Transition?

Clean energy technologies are often seen in their final products: electric vehicles, solar panels, wind turbines, or a stronger power grid. But behind these technologies are minerals, mines, processing hubs, workers, communities, ecosystems, and trade routes spread across the world. MineralSense helps make these connections visible. It traces how critical minerals move through global supply chains and highlights the social, environmental, and governance questions that come with them.

Explore the minerals behind clean energy, the places they come from, and the risks that are often hidden from view

Why This Matters Now

Rising Demand

Clean energy growth is increasing demand for critical minerals.

Concentrated Supply

Some minerals are produced or processed in only a few countries.

Local Impacts

Mining and processing can affect water, land, health, labour, and ecosystems.

Hidden Dependencies

Many clean technologies depend on supply chains most users never see.

Stories from the Ground

Critical minerals are often discussed in terms of numbers: reserves, demand, prices, and supply security. But behind those numbers are real places and people.

Here we bring together stories, investigations, and reports from mineral-producing regions. Explore how cobalt, lithium, nickel, copper, graphite, and other materials connect clean technologies to questions of water, labour, health, land, governance, and accountability.

'Staggering' Rise in Women With Reproductive Health Issues Near DRC Cobalt Mines

A Raid and Afrewatch investigation reveals reports of miscarriages, infections and birth defects among women and girls in mining communities. More than half of 144 interviewees raised concerns about reproductive health, linked to contact with contaminated water.

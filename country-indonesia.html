Indonesia

Risk Level: High • Nickel, Cobalt • Environmental damage, labor rights

Overview

Indonesia is the world's largest nickel producer and a growing cobalt supplier, positioning itself as a key EV battery hub. The government's push to ban raw nickel exports and develop domestic smelting has attracted massive Chinese investment. Villages have been displaced, rivers polluted, and workers face hazardous conditions. Human Rights Watch has documented serious abuses in the nickel mining industry. The rapid expansion has raised concerns over deforestation, tailings risks, and labor rights violations.

Map & Major Mines

Major mining areas: Sulawesi (Morowali, Weda Bay), Maluku (Halmahera), Papua.

Major Players

Companies: Tsingshan Holding, GEM, CATL, Vale Indonesia, Harita Group, Weda Bay Nickel
Governing Bodies: Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources, provincial governments, BKPM (investment board)
